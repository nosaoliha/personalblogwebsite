Dr.(Mrs.) Oludan Ibadan

Born March 13th, 1953

Website

www.oludan.com

Oludan is an African clothing store that promotes and sells a wide variety of ethically sourced African clothing, African accessories and African home decor.

Biography

Dr. Oludan has always loved fashion and wanted to do something African fashion related for the longest. Growing up under the wings of an African fashionista mother (from Ghana and Nigeria) carved the style crave in me from day one. Her bazaar of African print clothing always fascinated and inspired me. I grew up around them and today they are almost growing on everything around me.

Aside from being aesthetic pieces of clothing in my life, African prints played a pivotal role in encouraging me on the path of being the confident woman I am today.

She was born with a conspicuous red birthmark on the left side of my face. In Elementary school, kids often teased and bullied me verbally. They referred to her as a “Red face monster” or “Your-mother-must-have-slapped-you-with-a-red-spoon.” These negative remarks stabbed her self-worth to the point she believed she was truly a hideous monster. This ordeal became the genesis of her traumatic odyssey in the world of self-rejection and timidity. she did not have a sense of belonging among her peers and felt all alone.

Her darling mother, (her inspiration, embodiment of strength and biggest cheerleader) always encouraged and consoled her. She repeatedly declared to her “You are going to be great,” “You are beautiful” and “You are somebody.” For the longest, she didn’t believe her – after all, she was her mother and wouldn’t tell her otherwise.
